About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in paleoclimatology at IGE, Grenoble. My work focuses on reconstructing past ocean dynamics and climate variability using marine sediment records and climate model simulations.

I completed my PhD at Paris-Saclay University, investigating the variability of Iceland-Scotland Overflow Water over the last 400,000 years. I’m passionate about understanding Earth’s past Interglacial periods to improve our understanding of climate and ocean changes.

Research interests and skills

  • Proxy-based ocean reconstructions

    Analyzing marine sediment cores (various proxies).

  • Paleoclimate data compilation & harmoziation

    Searching, preprocessing, harmonizing and compiling ensemble of data.

  • Python-based data analysis & visualization

    Programming advanced python modules to analyze and visualize data.

  • Climate model and data-assimilation

    Using data to constrain climate models (incoming).
