About me

I am a postdoctoral researcher in paleoclimatology at IGE, Grenoble. My work focuses on reconstructing past ocean dynamics and climate variability using marine sediment records and climate model simulations.

I completed my PhD at Paris-Saclay University, investigating the variability of Iceland-Scotland Overflow Water over the last 400,000 years. I’m passionate about understanding Earth’s past Interglacial periods to improve our understanding of climate and ocean changes.

Research interests and skills

  • Proxy-based ocean reconstructions

    Analyzing marine sediment cores (various proxies).

  • Paleoclimate data compilation & harmoziation

    Searching, preprocessing, harmonizing and compiling ensemble of data.

  • Python-based data analysis & visualization

    Programming advanced python modules to analyze and visualize data.

  • Climate model and data-assimilation

    Using data to constrain climate models (incoming).

Portfolio

  • 2025 - SST compilation (MIS 9)

    Paper (preprint)

    A study compiling 98 Sea-Surface Temperature records, harmonizing chronologies and proxy- based calibrations, and process a Monte-Carlo routine to estimate global and regional temperature changes. This represents a future benchmark to assess the climate models's ability to reproduce past warm climates.

  • 2024 - Climate and Impacts

    Conference

    Participation (poster) to the 8th "Climate and Impacts" conference. I presented my preliminary results from my work at IGE (SST compilations).

  • 2024 - Deep Ocean dynamics over the last 400,000 years

    Paper

    A paper which pave the way to future paleoclimatological studies. It details the results (multi-proxy, multi-sites) spanning the last 400,000 years and validate the use of these proxies to interpret past ISOW intensity changes.

  • 2024-2026 - HOTCLIM

    Project

    Participation to the MOPGA-HOTCLIM project (data compilations and data-model comparisons). This project is focused on the study of past warm periods, which present a polar warming comparable to that projected by 2100.

  • 2023 - PhD thesis (Fr)

    PhD Manuscript

    Three years of work: extensive literature, lab details, preliminary work. The manuscript is only available in French. This PhD was supported by the CEA, CNRS and as part of the French program LEFE - DECORATING at the Laboratoire des Sciences du Climat et de l'Environnement (LSCE).

  • 2023 - AMARYLLIS cruise

    Oceanographic cruise

    Participation to the AMARYLLIS-AMAGAS (Leg 2) cruise onboard the R/V Marion Dufresne II. The aim of this mission was to retrive sediment cores from the North-East Brazil. It was an awesome experience, both professional and personal! More information in the website.

  • 2023 - Grain-size processing

    Paper

    Development of a new method to deal with ice-rafted debris and isolated a "current sorted" fraction in grain-size spectrum.

  • IRD correction package

    Code (Matlab)

    This is the package to use if you want to merge your unmixing End-Members (see Stevenard et al., 2023 for more information about the method).

  • 2022 - Canadian glacier dynamics

    Paper

    Reconstructions of the Manson-Icefield (Nunavut, Canada) over the last ~20,000 years using a multi- proxy approach (grain-size, X-CT scan, µXRF, XRD).
